The Pew

A pew. A home. A hundred years of stories.

Kindle Edition · June 2026

A forgotten soul beneath a staircase. A family who never knew they were being watched. And an old church pew, rescued long ago, that has quietly kept a century of love.

The Reading Experience

Not read. Lived in.

A KC Charis novel is not something you race through. It is a room with the lamps lit — a seat kept warm for you.

Quiet on the surface
Still rooms, turning seasons, the English countryside in lamplight and rain. Beneath the quiet, everything that matters is happening.
Tender at the core
Characters you come to love the way you love neighbors: slowly, then entirely. Grief is honored here — and so is joy.
Warm at the last page
These stories end the way coming home feels: the door opens, the light is on, and someone is glad you arrived.

Recurring emotional themes — memory & time · family & belonging · the turning seasons · resilience · the countryside · the unseen witnesses among us

About the Author

A Pen Lighter Than a Footstep

KC Charis writes stories that feel like coming home — quiet, character-driven fiction where memory and belonging meet.

Drawn to the invisible threads that bind generations — the rooms we inherit, the objects that outlive us, the ordinary moments in which love quietly takes root — Charis writes with a sensibility shaped by the English countryside, the turning of the seasons, and the hush of old houses.

The Pew: A pew. A home. A hundred years of stories. is Charis’s debut novel. It introduces a forgotten soul beneath a staircase and the family who, without ever knowing it, teach him that home is not a place, but the people who leave their mark upon our hearts.

Every book that follows will keep the same promise: a different house, perhaps; a different century, maybe — but always the same homecoming.
